Evolution and structure of sensory domains

Protein domains are the functional units of proteins which can fold and evolve independently. Sensory domains function as signal sensors in signal transduction systems. They play important roles in bacterial virulence and human diseases.

Signal transduction systems in bacteria (Wuichet et al., 2010)

Per-Arnt-Ser (PAS) domains are the most ubiquitous sensory domains throughout the tree of life. They have versatile functions include signal sensing, signal transduction, protein-protein interaction, and cellular localization. Using computational and phylogenetic methods, I'm interested in how this domain family recognizes different ligands and cofactors.

I'm also interested in other sensory domains including Cache domain and GAF domain.
